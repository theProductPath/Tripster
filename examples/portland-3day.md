# Portland, OR — 3-Day Itinerary

**Pace**: Medium | **Focus**: Coffee + Vintage Shopping | **Dates**: March 15-17, 2026

---

## Day 1: Coffee + Vintage in SE Portland — Buckman / Hawthorne

**9:00am — Coffee**
Heart Coffee Roasters (Buckman)
2211 E Burnside St. Start with their espresso flight. Minimal, beautiful space with great morning light. Known for their light roasts.

**10:15am — Shopping** (12 min walk)
Red Light Clothing Exchange (Hawthorne)
3590 SE Hawthorne Blvd. Best vintage menswear selection in SE Portland. Plan for 45-60 min — two floors of well-curated vintage.

**11:30am — Shopping** (5 min walk)
Crossroads Trading Co. (Hawthorne)
3736 SE Hawthorne Blvd. More contemporary secondhand. Good for finding name-brand pieces at thrift prices.

**12:30pm — Lunch**
Explore Hawthorne Blvd for lunch options. Strong taco, Thai, and sandwich game on this strip.

**2:00pm — Coffee + Browse** (8 min walk)
Either/Or Cafe (Hawthorne)
Relax with an afternoon coffee. Good outdoor seating when weather allows.

**3:00pm — Shopping**
House of Vintage (Hawthorne)
3315 SE Hawthorne Blvd. Massive multi-vendor vintage mall. Treasure hunt energy — budget at least an hour.

### Alternates
- Swap Heart Coffee → Coava Coffee Roasters (SE Grand Ave) if you prefer pour-over focus
- Swap Red Light → Buffalo Exchange if you want faster browsing

## Day 2: Design + Coffee in NE Portland — Alberta / Mississippi

**9:30am — Coffee**
Upper Left Roasters (Alberta)
Multiple NE locations. Clean, modern space. Excellent drip coffee and pastries.

**10:45am — Shopping** (10 min walk)
Yo Vintage! (Alberta)
Well-curated vintage with strong menswear picks. Owner has great taste.

**11:45am — Browse** (walkable)
Alberta Arts District stroll — galleries, murals, and small design shops along Alberta St.

**12:30pm — Lunch**
Alberta has excellent food options. Strong pizza, ramen, and Southern food scene.

**2:00pm — Shopping** (15 min walk or short drive)
Mississippi Avenue shops
Cluster of vintage, home goods, and design stores along Mississippi Ave.

**3:30pm — Coffee**
Either coffee shop along Mississippi or head to Guilder for an afternoon pick-me-up.

### Alternates
- If Alberta feels quiet (some shops closed Mon/Tue), swap to Division/Clinton neighborhood
- Add Pistils Nursery (Mississippi) if you're into plants/design objects

## Day 3: Downtown + Pearl District — Central Portland

**9:00am — Coffee**
Stumptown Coffee Roasters (Downtown)
128 SW 3rd Ave. The original location. Worth visiting for the history even if you know the brand.

**10:00am — Books**
Powell's City of Books (Pearl)
1005 W Burnside St. A full city block of books. Go to the rare book room on the top floor.

**12:00pm — Lunch**
Pearl District / West Burnside options — strong ramen, pizza, and lunch counter scene.

**1:30pm — Shopping** (walkable)
Explore NW 23rd Ave shops — mix of boutiques, vintage, and design stores.

**3:00pm — Coffee**
Barista (NW 23rd or Pearl location)
Known for rotating guest roasters. A good final coffee stop.

**4:00pm — Flexible**
Wander the Pearl District galleries or walk along the waterfront.

### Alternates
- Swap Stumptown → Courier Coffee if you want something smaller and more indie
- Add Tender Loving Empire (downtown) for local design goods and music

## Neighborhood Notes

- **Buckman / Hawthorne (SE)**: The heart of Portland's vintage and coffee scene. Extremely walkable. Hawthorne Blvd is the main drag. Expect a 10-15 min walk between most stops.
- **Alberta / Mississippi (NE)**: More spread out but packed with character. Alberta has an arts walk vibe. Mississippi is more concentrated. Some shops keep shorter hours — check before heading out.
- **Downtown / Pearl**: More polished and urban. Powell's is the anchor. NW 23rd has good boutique shopping. Easy transit connections to other neighborhoods.

## Observations
- Portland's coffee scene is heavily concentrated in SE and NE neighborhoods
- Vintage shopping clusters naturally around Hawthorne, Alberta, and Mississippi
- Most shops open at 10-11am — plan coffee first, shopping second
- The city is very bikeable if you want to cover more ground between neighborhoods
