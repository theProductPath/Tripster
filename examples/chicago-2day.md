# Chicago, IL — 2-Day Itinerary

**Pace**: Maximal | **Focus**: Coffee + Vintage/Thrift Menswear | **Dates**: Feb 14-15, 2026 (Sat-Sun)

---

## Day 1 (Saturday Feb 14): West Loop Coffee + Wicker Park Vintage Corridor

The plan: start with world-class coffee in Fulton Market, grab the best designer menswear shop in the West Loop, then ride the Blue Line north to the Milwaukee Ave vintage strip — the densest cluster of shops in the city. Everything on Milwaukee is within a 10-minute walk of everything else.

**8:00am — Coffee**
☕ **Sawada Coffee** (West Loop)
112 N Green St. World-class latte art and a signature military latte. Espresso-forward program in a clean, stylish space. Opens at 8am.

**9:00am — Coffee** (15 min walk west on Fulton)
☕ **Metric Coffee** (Fulton Market)
2021 W Fulton St. Independent Chicago roaster with exceptional espresso and fresh Brite doughnuts. Roastery vibes. Closes at 3pm so hit it early.

**10:15am — Shopping** (walk back east, 3 min from Sawada)
🛍️ **Notre** (West Loop)
118 N Peoria St. Curated menswear blending streetwear and high-end fashion. Opens at 11am Sat — arrive a touch early and grab a walk around Fulton Market while you wait.

**11:00am — Transit to Wicker Park**
Take the Blue Line from Grand to Damen (~8 min) or a quick rideshare. Head to the south end of the Milwaukee Ave strip.

**11:15am — Shopping**
🛍️ **Bodega Vintage** (Wicker Park)
1284 N Milwaukee Ave. 10+ rotating vendors, always fresh inventory. Opens at 11am Sat. Start here at the south end and work your way north.

**12:00pm — Shopping** (5 min walk north)
🛍️ **Kokorokoko** (Wicker Park)
1323 N Milwaukee Ave. Chicago's best vintage shop for era-specific finds — 80s, 90s, Y2K. Beautifully sourced. Opens at noon.

**12:30pm — Shopping** (2 min walk)
🛍️ **Mildblend Supply Co.** (Wicker Park)
1342 N Milwaukee Ave. Curated menswear — raw denim, boots, bags. Made in USA focus. Right next to Kokorokoko.

**1:15pm — Shopping** (3 min walk north)
🛍️ **Store B Vintage** (Wicker Park)
1472 N Milwaukee Ave. High-end vintage — designer brands, every piece hand-selected and repaired. Open since 2001.

**2:00pm — Coffee Break** (1 min walk)
☕ **The Wormhole Coffee** (Wicker Park)
1462 N Milwaukee Ave. Quirky 80s-themed independent cafe. Strong espresso. Recharge here before heading out. Open until 7pm Sat.

### Walk-by Option
**Vintage House Chicago** (1433 N Milwaukee) is right on the strip between Mildblend and Store B. Weekend format features 12 rotating vendors — pop in if something catches your eye.

### Day 1 Alternates
- Swap Notre → **Independence Chicago** (1909 W Division) if you want to frontload the menswear shopping — best curated menswear boutique in the city (Engineered Garments, Kapital, OrSlow)
- Swap Bodega → **Buffalo Exchange** (1478 N Milwaukee) if you prefer faster buy-sell-trade browsing
- Valentine's Day evening is free — Wicker Park and West Loop both have great dinner options

## Day 2 (Sunday Feb 15): West Town Coffee Crawl + Vintage Deep Cuts

The plan: hit four coffee spots across West Town, Ukrainian Village, and Bucktown in the early morning while the shops are still closed, then pivot to the best vintage shops that have Sunday hours — including Chicago's only all-men's vintage shop.

**7:00am — Coffee**
☕ **Big Shoulders Coffee** (Noble Square / West Town)
1105 W Chicago Ave. No-nonsense independent roaster. Opens at 7am Sun. Quiet start.

**8:15am — Coffee** (10 min walk south)
☕ **Dark Matter Coffee — The Mothership** (Ukrainian Village)
738 N Western Ave. Flagship roaster HQ. Barrel-aged beans, experimental espresso, bold creative energy. Opens at 6am.

**9:30am — Coffee** (rideshare or 15 min walk north)
☕ **Ipsento 606** (Bucktown)
1813 N Milwaukee Ave. Creative signature drinks — coconut cream, honey, cayenne. Near the 606 elevated trail. Opens at 7am.

**10:30am — Coffee** (12 min walk northwest)
☕ **Hexe Coffee Co.** (Bucktown)
2000 W Diversey Pkwy. Barrel-aged and experimental coffees. Open until 8pm so it's also a fallback afternoon stop. Opens at 7:30am.

**11:30am — Shopping** (rideshare south to Division St)
🛍️ **Independence Chicago** (Wicker Park)
1909 W Division St. If you didn't hit it Day 1: premier menswear boutique. Engineered Garments, Kapital, OrSlow, Gitman Vintage. Opens 11am Sun.

**12:15pm — Shopping** (rideshare west to North Ave)
🛍️ **Richard's Fabulous Finds** (Humboldt Park / West Town)
2545 W North Ave. Chicago's only all-men's vintage shop. Victorian era through the 1970s — suits, coats, hats, ties, dress shoes. Incredible curation. Opens 12pm Sun.

**1:30pm — Shopping** (rideshare east to Chicago Ave)
🛍️ **Dovetail** (West Town)
1452 W Chicago Ave. Curated vintage from the 1950s-1990s — clothing, housewares, art, barware. Every piece laundered or dry cleaned. Opens 12pm Sun.

**2:30pm — Shopping** (walk or rideshare to Milwaukee Ave)
🛍️ **Buffalo Exchange** (Wicker Park)
1478 N Milwaukee Ave. Easy browsing to close out the trip. Open until 8pm Sun if you want to extend.

## Neighborhood Notes

**West Loop / Fulton Market**: Chicago's culinary and design district. Sawada, Metric, and Notre are all within a few blocks. Excellent restaurant options for dinner. Easy Blue Line access.

**Wicker Park / Milwaukee Ave**: The vintage shopping backbone. 8+ shops within a 0.4-mile stretch of Milwaukee Ave between roughly 1284 and 1478 N Milwaukee. Flat, walkable, and the walks between shops are so short you barely feel the cold.

**Bucktown / Diversey**: North of Wicker Park, connected by Milwaukee Ave. Ipsento 606 is the natural bridge between the two areas. More spread out but strong coffee options.

**West Town / Ukrainian Village / Noble Square**: Gritty, authentic neighborhoods west of downtown. Big Shoulders, Dark Matter Mothership, and Dovetail are scattered here. Moderate walkability — short rideshares help in February.

**Humboldt Park**: Richard's Fabulous Finds is the reason to come here. Worth the trip for the only all-men's vintage shop in the city.

## Dropped Venues (Worth a Detour If You Have Extra Time)

- **Lost Eras** (Rogers Park) — 15,000 sq ft, million+ items, largest vintage collection in the Midwest. But it's 35+ min north of everything else and closed Sunday. Only works if you sacrifice Day 1 morning.
- **Anticonquista Cafe** (Pilsen) — Chicago's first farm-owned roaster. Beautiful story, but Pilsen is disconnected from the main corridor.
- **Gaslight Coffee / Dayglow** (Logan Square) — Both excellent, but Logan Square adds transit friction without enough shops to pair with.
- **Intelligentsia** (Loop) — The OG third-wave spot, but you'll get better independent experiences at the shops on this list.

## Observations

- Milwaukee Ave in Wicker Park is the most efficient shopping corridor in Chicago — 8 high-quality vintage/design shops within a 10-minute walk
- Chicago's coffee scene is deep and independent-roaster driven. You could do a coffee-only trip and not repeat a shop
- February weather means short outdoor stretches between warm indoor shops. The Milwaukee Ave density is your best friend
- Saturday has the widest shop availability; several spots are closed Monday/Tuesday but open weekends
- Richard's Fabulous Finds is the sleeper hit — the only all-men's vintage shop in the city
